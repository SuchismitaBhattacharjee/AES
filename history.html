1935

The Dawn of a Dream

It was one morning in 1935 when Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel called a meeting with three spirited visionaries — G. V. Mavlankar (who would later become the first Speaker of the Lok Sabha), Sheth Kasturbhai Lalbhai (an industrialist and philanthropist), and Sheth Amritlal Hargovandas (a renowned industrialist). Their concern was simple yet urgent — the state of higher education in Ahmedabad.

At that time, Gujarat College — the only institution for higher learning in the city, established by the British Government in 1861 — offered just two faculties: Arts and Science. Under colonial control, students and teachers were denied freedom of thought and national expression. Many ambitious students had to travel to Bombay for further education.

That meeting, inspired by Sardar Patel’s patriotic vision, sparked a movement that would change Gujarat's educational landscape forever. Soon after, prominent citizens like C.C. Gandhi, Anandshanker Dhruv, Harilal Kapadia, Jivanlal Diwan, and Balvantrai Thakore joined hands. Together, they dreamed of an educational organization that would nurture not only intellect but also national pride — and thus, the Ahmedabad Education Society (AES) was born.

Looking for Money & Meaning

Ahmedabad Education Society was officially established in 1935, with a draft prepared by G. V. Mavlankar. Dr. Anandshanker Dhruv became its first President, and Sheth Kasturbhai Lalbhai served as the Treasurer. But the founders wanted more than funds — they wanted involvement, belief, and participation.

Instead of relying on elite circles for donations, these respected leaders personally took to the streets of Ahmedabad, collecting contributions door-to-door with bags in hand. They were not merely seeking money — they were kindling a spirit of reform. Kasturbhai Lalbhai and his peers wanted to turn philanthropists into active nation-builders, uniting citizens under the shared goal of advancing education in Gujarat.

Within months, AES registered over 300 members — a symbol of the community's trust and enthusiasm. Contributions from industrialists and citizens alike soon followed, laying a strong foundation of both funds and values.

“As far as your eyes can see…”

- Kasturbhai Lalbhai's vision for the land

And when the time came to purchase land for setting up new institutions, someone asked Kasturbhai how much land ought to be purchased. His reply was simple yet visionary.Such was his foresight and passion for the spread of education and academic excellence in Gujarat. Acting on his own words, he went on to acquire nearly 700 acres of land — when only ten acres were needed.

This single decision not only shaped the physical landscape of Ahmedabad’s academic institutions but also laid the groundwork for what would one day become the educational hub of Gujarat.

Present Day

A Legacy That Lives On

What began as a patriotic initiative in 1935 is today an educational ecosystem that shapes the future of thousands. With over eight decades of excellence, the Ahmedabad Education Society stands tall as a registered Public Charitable Trust — a proud representation of Ahmedabad’s citizens and their enduring belief in the power of education.

Today, AES is home to some of the city’s most prestigious institutions across diverse fields — Arts, Commerce, Science, Pharmacy, Architecture, Planning, Management, and Technology. It is more than a society; it is a movement — one that continues to honor the vision of its founders by empowering generations to think freely, act boldly, and serve the nation with knowledge and integrity
